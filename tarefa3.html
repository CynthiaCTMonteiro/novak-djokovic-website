Novak Djokovic

Novak Djokovic is a Serbian professional tennis player. He was born on May 22, 1987 in Belgrade, Yugoslavia.

Djokovic has won 20 Grand Slam singles titles, tied for the most in history with Rafael Nadal and Roger Federer. He has won the Australian Open nine times, Wimbledon five times, the US Open three times, and the French Open three times.

Djokovic has been ranked world No. 1 by the Association of Tennis Professionals (ATP) a record total of 332 weeks. He is considered one of the greatest tennis players of all time.

Personal Life

Djokovic is married to Jelena Djokovic, and they have three children together: Stefan, Tara, and Aleksandar.

Playing Style

Djokovic is known for his all-around game, with excellent groundstrokes, movement, and defensive skills. He is also known for his mental toughness and ability to come back from difficult situations.

One of Djokovic's signature shots is his backhand, which he can hit with both power and precision. He is also a strong server and has excellent footwork, allowing him to cover the court effectively.

Records and Achievements

  • 20 Grand Slam singles titles
  • 36 ATP Tour Masters 1000 titles
  • 5 ATP Finals titles
  • Record for most weeks ranked world No. 1 (332 weeks)
  • Completed career Grand Slam (winning all four Grand Slam titles)
  • Golden Masters (winning all nine ATP Tour Masters 1000 events)
  • Olympic bronze medalist (singles, 2008 Beijing Olympics)
